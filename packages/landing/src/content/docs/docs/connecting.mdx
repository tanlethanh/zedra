title: Connect your phone
description: Pair the Zedra app with your desktop by scanning a QR code, and understand how the encrypted P2P tunnel works.
---

Once the daemon is running, pairing takes one scan.

## Pair by scanning the QR code

1. Run `zedra start --detach` in your project directory. It prints a QR code.
2. Open the Zedra app and tap **Scan QR**.
3. Point the camera at the QR code.

That's it. The app remembers the pairing, so you only scan once per workspace.
Each `zedra start` directory shows up as its own workspace in the app.

The QR code carries your machine's public key and a one-time pairing secret —
not your network address. Routing information is resolved fresh each time you
connect, so the pairing keeps working even after your IP changes.

## How the tunnel works

Zedra opens an end-to-end encrypted connection (QUIC with TLS 1.3) between your
phone and your desktop. There are two paths:

- **Direct (preferred).** Your phone and desktop discover each other and punch a
  direct path through your network. Traffic flows device-to-device with nothing
  in between.
- **Relay fallback.** When a direct path can't be established — for example
  behind a symmetric NAT or strict firewall — traffic routes through a Zedra
  relay instead. The relay only forwards encrypted packets; it can't decrypt or
  inspect your code, terminal output, or files.

Even while on a relay, Zedra keeps trying for a direct path in the background. If
one opens up, the connection migrates onto it automatically.

## Staying connected

The app pings the daemon to track the connection and shows round-trip latency in
the transport badge. If the network drops, it reconnects automatically with a
short backoff. If your desktop restarts, re-run `zedra start` and the app
reconnects on its own.

If you can't connect at all, see [Troubleshooting](/docs/troubleshooting/).
